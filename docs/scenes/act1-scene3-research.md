# Scene 3: Research and Reflection

**Location:** Living room couch
**Characters:** [[rachel|Rachel]]
**Script label:** `act1_research`

## Summary

That evening, Rachel researches bird behavior while drinking vodka and Red Bull, feeling drowsy despite the caffeine. She obsesses over why a crow — too intelligent for such mistakes — would crash into her window. She knows she's overdone the drinking and will pay for it with a multi-day hangover. She sleeps on the couch with TV on, mixing pills with vodka, telling herself it's temporary.

## Key Beats

- Rachel researches bird behaviour on the couch, mixing vodka with Red Bull
- Caffeine isn't doing anything. Neither is the liquor. She knows she's overdone it today.
- The image of the crow shattering the window keeps replaying in her mind. The sound of it.
- Crows are too smart for that. They remember faces. They hold grudges. Unless it was sick. Unless it was something else entirely.
- She hesitates before taking another swig and swallowing another pill. But she tells herself it's temporary.
- {i}She'd told herself that before.{/i}
