# The Entity

**Role:** True antagonist — the thing wearing the toys

**The short answer:** A death-adjacent entity with no fixed form, no name, and no agenda beyond proximity to dying. Predates gods, demons, moral frameworks. It has no personality — it has *appetites*.

**What it is NOT:**
- A demon (predates moral frameworks)
- A god (no worshippers, no temples, no name)
- A ghost (doesn't remember being human)
- A curse (doesn't need permission)
- A possession (doesn't crawl inside — it just finds you)

**What it IS:**
- A passenger
- A mirror — reflects the death it finds
- A continuation — finishes what dying starts

## The "Bargain" Question

Ethan thought he made a deal. He didn't. The entity can't honor anything — it just follows the beacon home. Ethan died believing he'd saved his family. He hadn't.

## Why Rachel?

She is death-adjacent through accumulation — alcohol, pills, choosing death incrementally. The entity doesn't care about her feelings. It cares about the threshold. She's been standing on it for a year.
