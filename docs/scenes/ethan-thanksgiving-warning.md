# SCENE: The Warning (Thanksgiving — One Year Before)

*A breadcrumb scene. Lives in Rachel's memory as a drunk moment she's ashamed of. Read again after the crow reveal, it crystallizes.*

---

## THEN: Thanksgiving Night

The apartment smelled like dry turkey and white wine. Tommy was three, already in his dinosaur pajamas, half-asleep on the couch. Rachel was in the kitchen, scraping plates, not thinking about anything in particular.

She heard the window open behind her.

When she turned, Ethan was standing on the sill. Not drunkenly — *experimentally*. Like he was testing the weight of the second story. Like he was calculating the fall.

"Ethan. What the fuck are you doing."

He looked at her. And for a half-second — just a half-second — something in his face *shifted*. Like a mask slipping. Like someone looking out from behind Ethan's eyes.

"Tell me one more time," he said. His voice was his. Mostly. "Tell me you love me."

"You've been drinking—"

"*Tell me.*"

"I love you." She said it the way you say it when you're annoyed and also terrified and also so tired of男人的 bullshit. "Now get down from there."

He smiled. Not his smile. The crow's smile — a thing that knew exactly what it was doing and was delighted by it.

"Good," he said. "That's good. Because I'm going to tell you something, and you're not going to hear it. You're going to hear *drunk Ethan* and you're going to be mad at me, and that's fine. That's better than—"

He stopped. Cocked his head. Listening to something she couldn't hear.

"Than what?" she asked.

He shook his head. Whatever moment he'd been having passed. The mask settled back into place. Ethan was back.

"Forget it," he said. "I'm just fucked up. Pre-deployment drinking. You know how it is."

"You have two weeks before you deploy again. You're not—"

"I know what I said." His voice was firm. Final. Then, softer: "Keep Tommy away from my things. My clothes. My books. Anything of mine in this house. Promise me."

"You're being dramatic. You're drunk."

"I'm always drunk." He laughed. It was his laugh. The real one. "I'm going to be a lot more drunk before this is over."

"What does that—"

"HEY!" David from down the hall — neighbor, drinking buddy — was in the doorway with a bottle of something. "Are we doing the dare or are we talking to our wives? Come on, Mountain Goat. Show us your reflexes."

Ethan winked at Rachel. A real wink. A *he's-gone* wink.

"Yeah," he said. "Let's see how the goat climbs."

---

## THEN: The Fall

They heard it from the kitchen. Not a scream. A *dismissal* — like the universe saying *no, actually, you don't get to climb out of this*.

Rachel ran to the window. Ethan's body was on the pavement below, crumpled in a way that bodies aren't supposed to crumple. The neighbor was already screaming. Tommy was crying.

And on the windowsill, where Ethan had been standing, there was a single black feather.

She didn't notice it until the paramedics were loading her into the ambulance, the neighbor watching Tommy, her hands shaking too hard to function.

A feather. On the sill.

She didn't remember it being there before.

## NOW: Rachel's Memory (Reprise)

*(This lives in the manuscript as Rachel remembering the fall. But in the original draft, it's just guilt and grief. After the crow reveal, she reads it differently.)*

She'll think about that feather later. In the hospital. In the weeks after. In the months of drinking that followed the weeks of not eating. In the quiet moments when Tommy was at school and the apartment was too empty and she could hear Ethan's voice saying *keep him away from my things*.

She'll think: that was a crow feather.

She'll think: there were no crows on the sixth floor.

She'll think: he knew. Some part of him knew what was coming. And he tried to warn me.

And she'll hate herself — for not listening, for being annoyed, for hearing *drunk Ethan* instead of *dying Ethan*. For being so fucking tired of his bullshit that she couldn't hear what he was actually saying.

*Keep Tommy away from my things.*

The toys arrived a year later. Addressed to Captain Branson.

## REVEAL TRIGGER

And he was trying to push the crow's attention away from his family when it happened.
