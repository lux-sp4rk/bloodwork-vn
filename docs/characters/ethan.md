# Captain Ethan Branson (deceased)

**Role:** Deceased — Rachel's late husband, Tommy's father

**Background:** Army captain, rose through ranks quickly, came from poor "trailer trash" background, three deployments to the Middle East.

**Physical:** Shorter than Rachel, commanding presence.

**Death:** Fell from second-story window at Thanksgiving, one year before story events. Officially a drunken accident.

---

## The Truth (Act 3 revelation)

On deployment, Ethan found something — a fetish, an idol, an object buried somewhere it had no business being. Old enough to have been waiting. He thought it was a souvenir. He brought it home. Put it on a shelf.

**The fetish is a beacon.** The entity has been circling the house since Ethan brought it home, reading the family, waiting. Ethan figured it out eventually — the house felt wrong when the lights were off, he couldn't sleep, something said *this isn't yours anymore*. He tried to get rid of it. It came back. It was his.

**The mountain goat line:** Ethan bragging about his climbing skills — but underneath, he knew. He knew he was going to fall. The entity doesn't kill directly — it just makes you climb where you shouldn't, jump when you shouldn't.

**The Thanksgiving window:** Either Ethan trying to destroy the fetish by destroying himself, or the entity already pushing him toward the window because the signal was stronger outside, closer to the birds. He fell. The fetish stayed. The entity settled in.

**What Ethan tried to do:** "Keep Tommy away from my things." He meant the fetish. Rachel heard *clothes*. He couldn't say it directly.

**The Thanksgiving Warning:** [[scenes/ethan-thanksgiving-warning|Full scene (scenes/ethan-thanksgiving-warning.md)]] — Ethan on the sill, calculating the fall, trying to warn Rachel. Reads differently after the chatbot reveal. Key breadcrumb scene.

**The tragedy:** Ethan died believing he'd saved his family. He hadn't. There was no bargain to make. The entity just followed the beacon home.

**Rachel's unconscious knowledge:** She married a man who came back with something in his bag. She's been trying to drink the wrongness out of herself ever since.
