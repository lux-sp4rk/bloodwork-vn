# SCENE: The Warning (Thanksgiving — One Year Before)

*A breadcrumb scene. Lives in Rachel's memory as a drunk moment she's ashamed of. Read again after the crow reveal, it crystallizes.*

---

## THEN: Thanksgiving Night

The apartment smelled like dry turkey and white wine. Tommy was three, already in his dinosaur pajamas, half-asleep on the couch. Rachel was in the kitchen, scraping plates, not thinking about anything in particular.

On the shelf behind her — the shelf where she and Ethan kept the weird stuff, the travel souvenirs, the things neither of them could throw away — there was a small idol. Ethan had brought it back from his last deployment. Something he'd found. Something he'd said was *just cool, look at this thing, Rach*.

She didn't like it. She'd never said so. It sat on the shelf and she worked around it, the way you work around a piece of furniture that's in the wrong place but isn't worth moving.

She heard the window open behind her.

When she turned, Ethan was standing on the sill. Not drunkenly — *experimentally*. Like he was testing the weight of the second story. Like he was calculating the fall.

"Ethan. What the fuck are you doing."

He looked at her. And for a half-second — just a half-second — something in his face *shifted*. Like a man watching himself from very far away.

"Tell me one more time," he said. His voice was his. Mostly. "Tell me you love me."

"You've been drinking—"

"*Tell me.*"

"I love you." She said it the way you say it when you're annoyed and also terrified and also so tired of男人的 bullshit. "Now get down from there."

He smiled. Not quite his smile. Like someone had studied his smile and was trying it on.

"Good," he said. "That's good. Because I'm going to tell you something, and you're not going to hear it. You're going to hear *drunk Ethan* and you're going to be mad at me, and that's fine."

"What does that—"

"Keep Tommy away from my things."

She blinked. "Your... what?"

"My things. My clothes. My books. The stuff in my bag." He paused. Something in him — the part that was still Ethan, still the man who loved her — pushed harder. "Don't touch anything I brought home from deployment. Don't let Tommy near it. Promise me."

"You're being dramatic. You're drunk."

"I'm always drunk." He laughed. It was his laugh. The real one. "I'm going to be a lot more drunk before this is over."

"That's not funny."

"No," he agreed. "It isn't."

He looked at the shelf. At the small idol sitting there, catching the light. Then he looked back at Rachel.

"Forget it," he said. Whatever moment he'd been having passed. Ethan was back — just a man on a sill who drank too much and was about to do something stupid. "I'm just fucked up. Pre-deployment drinking. You know how it is."

"You have two weeks before you deploy again. You're not—"

"I know what I said." His voice was firm. Final. Then, softer: "Just... keep him away from my things. Please."

---

## THEN: The Fall

They heard it from the kitchen. Not a scream. A *dismissal* — like the universe saying *no, actually, you don't get to climb out of this*.

Rachel ran to the window. Ethan's body was on the pavement below, crumpled in a way that bodies aren't supposed to crumple. The neighbor was already screaming. Tommy was crying.

And on the windowsill, where Ethan had been standing, there was a single black feather.

She didn't notice it until the paramedics were loading her into the ambulance, the neighbor watching Tommy, her hands shaking too hard to function.

A feather. On the sill.

She didn't remember it being there before.

## NOW: Rachel's Memory (Reprise)

*(This lives in the manuscript as Rachel remembering the fall. In the original draft, it's just guilt and grief. After the crow reveal, she reads it differently.)*

She'll think about that feather later. In the hospital. In the weeks after. In the months of drinking that followed the weeks of not eating. In the quiet moments when Tommy was at school and the apartment was too empty and she could hear Ethan's voice saying *keep Tommy away from my things*.

She'll think: that was a crow feather.

She'll think: there were no crows on the sixth floor.

She'll think: he knew. Some part of him knew what was coming. And he tried to warn me.

And she'll hate herself — for not listening, for being annoyed, for hearing *drunk Ethan* instead of a dying man trying to save his son. For being so fucking tired of his bullshit that she couldn't hear what he was actually saying.

*Keep Tommy away from my things.*

The fetish was on the shelf. She'd walked past it every day for a year. She'd dusted around it. She'd never touched it.

Tommy had touched it once. When Ethan first brought it home. He'd picked it up and she remembered him saying *cool, what's this Dad?* and Ethan had grabbed it out of his hand fast, too fast, said *nothing, just Dad's stuff, don't touch*.

She hadn't thought about that in months.

She thought about it now.
